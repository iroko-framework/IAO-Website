Governance & Transparency Ilé Aña Olofí, Inc. is a Louisiana nonprofit corporation recognized by the Internal Revenue Service as a 501(c)(3) public charity. The organization supports religious, cultural, educational, preservation, and community-based work rooted in Afro-Atlantic sacred traditions. The Iroko Historical Society is a sponsored archival and public humanities initiative of Ilé Aña Olofí, Inc. Ilé Aña Olofí provides the legal, fiscal, and organizational structure through which IHS carries out preservation, research, metadata development, and public education work. Ilé Aña Olofí, Inc. is a founder-led nonprofit organization with board oversight and internal recordkeeping. Its governance model is intentionally small in order to preserve religious integrity, privacy, accountability, and continuity of stewardship. Public documents available upon request or through public records include the organization’s IRS determination letter, articles of incorporation, and annual IRS filing information, including Form 990-N, 990-EZ, or Form 990 as applicable. Board minutes, internal resolutions, donor records, contributor agreements, ceremonial records, restricted archival records, and sensitive cultural or religious materials are maintained internally and are not published online. Grant, partnership, and due diligence inquiries may be directed to: contact@ileanaolofi.org.
